# Simulation-Based Evaluation of CRR

> **Naming.** This is a *simulation-based* evaluation, not a "real-world" one. The
> operating envelope is generated by an AR(1) weather process; it has not been fitted
> to or validated against observed meteorological data. See *Threats to validity*.

This document supersedes the study in [`evaluation.md`](evaluation.md) for RQ1 and RQ3.
It exists because the earlier study's design could not answer the questions it posed:
several of its headline results were consequences of how the experiment was
constructed rather than measurements of CRR. Those defects are catalogued in
§1 because they explain every design decision that follows.

## 1. Why the earlier study had to be replaced

Each item below was verified by running the code, not by reading it.

| # | Defect | Evidence |
|---|--------|----------|
| 1 | **The Type-III refinement is a tautology.** `refine_type_III` is `base(cfg,task) * (1+delta)` — a *uniform* scale. `argmin_k Σ_j E[k,j]` is scale-invariant, so the ranking is preserved for **any** delta. | Identical config ranking at delta = 0.05, 0.22, 0.60, 5.0 and **100.0**; per-config cost-ratio spread `4.4e-16` (machine zero). ### 1.1 The finding underneath all of them

**There is no expensive optimizer to avoid at the evaluated scale.**

- One "expensive MILP call": **4.89 ms**. The *entire 16-entry cache* revalidates in **78 ms**.
- The MILP being avoided: **20 binary variables, 9 rows**.
- The self-contained branch-and-bound was **2.08× slower than `scipy`/HiGHS cold-solving the same instance**.

CRR carries certificates, a reverse index and zonotopes to avoid a 5 ms call, using
Stage-2 machinery that costs the same 35 energy evaluations as the call itself. The
mechanism is not at fault; it was being demonstrated far below the scale at which it
could matter.

## 2. Setup

### 2.1 An operating envelope, not a grid

The ODD is a simulated weather process — AR(1) persistence, diurnal cycles and
sporadic fronts — over a **declared grid of climatologies** (Berlin summer/winter,
Bergen autumn, Madrid summer, Reykjavík spring, …). Each seed draws a *different
environment*, so between-seed variance reflects different operating conditions rather
than redrawn noise around one hand-written setting.

Cache entries are sampled **stratified over the wind quantiles of the visited trace**,
with visit frequency carried as a reported weight. Selecting the *most-visited* cells
instead would concentrate the cache on the climatological mode — precisely where the
optimum does not move — and would silently cancel the physics.

Measured effective dimensionality (PCA of the standardized trace): **[0.36, 0.33, 0.31]**
— three genuinely independent axes. The original linspace cache is `[1.0, 0, 0]`.

### 2.2 Physics that can move the optimum

Only channels that measurably reach the optimum are modelled:

- **Wind is the sole re-ranking channel.** Propulsion is driven by *airspeed*, task
  time by *ground speed*, so `dE/dw = 2B(v+w)/v · L/3600` **depends on v**: a headwind
  penalises slow configurations disproportionately. Wind is applied **as a vector per
  leg**, so an out-and-back route does not cancel — the headwind leg is flown for
  longer than the tailwind leg. Measured: the argmin migrates across the wind envelope
  in **90% of configuration draws** (the uniform-scale model: **0%, by construction**).
- **Temperature is a feasibility channel only**, by construction: it derates usable
  capacity and air density but never enters `config_cost`, so it can flip feasibility
  and never the ranking. Modelled with a *bounded, non-monotone* derate (real LiPo
  capacity peaks at 20–35 °C). An Arrhenius factor was rejected: it is monotone
  increasing and would predict >100% of nameplate at 50 °C.
- **Humidity is excluded from the energy model.** Over the RH span the cache covers it
  changes power by <0.1% — three orders of magnitude below the differences that decide
  the argmin — and it is usually stated with the sign backwards (moist air is *less*
  dense). Including it would be physics-washing. *(The comment at `refinement.py:82`,
  "denser moist air aloft", has this backwards.)*
- **Visibility is not an ODD dimension**: in the trace generator it is a deterministic
  function of humidity and wind (R² ≈ 0.87) and enters no physics.

### 2.3 A refinement that is a fidelity step, not a dial

Type III is **M0 → M1** on the energy model: M0 is the existing published
parasitic-drag model (`P ~ v_air²`); M1 is the standard rotary-wing model
(Zeng, Zhang & Lim, IEEE TWC 2019), which **adds** the induced-power branch that
dominates in slow flight. Nothing is deleted to manufacture the step. The two differ
**non-uniformly** across configurations — measured cost-ratio spread **0.40**, versus
`4.4e-16` for the current uniform operator — and it re-ranks in **77%** of draws.

Type II is a regulatory reserve increase (a pure RHS change: it can only flip
feasibility, never the ranking — in Track A its success is therefore *definitional,
not evidence*). Type I is payload mass, entering both the objective and the feasible set.

Refinements carry an **ODD scope** (e.g. a reserve hike only above 8 m/s wind).
Without scoping, every footprint is the whole cache and `footprint_resolve` is
bit-identical to `full_resolve` — which would make the fair baseline vacuous.

### 2.4 Two tracks

- **Track A — the degenerate case we inherited.** Energy is agent-independent, so cost
  is config-determined and "solving" is a scan over K numbers plus a feasibility check.
  Under vector-wind physics its energy matrix becomes rank **2** rather than rank 1
  (wind direction × task heading is a genuine task-level term), but the second
  component carries ~0.7% of the spectrum, so it remains effectively a scan.
  **Track A is a correctness control and carries no efficiency number.**
- **Track B — the assignment-coupled fleet.** Heterogeneous agents (battery
  state-of-health, home bases with transit legs) make `E[i,k,j]` agent-dependent, so
  the objective genuinely couples to the assignment and the problem is a real MILP.
  Uses **aggregated** config linking (`Σ_{i,j} x[i,j,k] ≤ M·J·y_k`, K rows) rather than
  the disaggregated form in `milp_solver.py` (M·J·K rows), which is ~90× slower here
  because the engine's cost is row-dominated. Single-trip assignment only; chaining
  would make this an energy-constrained VRP.

### 2.5 A faster engine, so the premise is real

The self-contained engine was rebuilt so that the evaluation can run at a scale where a
re-solve is genuinely expensive, without abandoning the no-external-solver property:

- one LU factorization per iteration reused across all three solves (was three separate
  `O(m³)` solves per pivot); Dantzig pricing with a Bland fallback on stalling;
  vectorized ratio tests;
- branch-and-bound: **reliability branching** (pseudocost + strong branching on
  unreliable candidates), **hybrid best-first + plunging** node selection, and an
  LP-guided **dive heuristic** for an early incumbent. The heuristic is only ever used
  as an incumbent for pruning — never reported as a proven optimum.

| fleet | binary vars | before | after | vs HiGHS |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4 × 5 × 7 | 147 | did not finish in 5 min | **383 ms** | 11.6× |
| 5 × 6 × 8 | 248 | 109 s | **200 ms** | 12.3× |
| 6 × 8 × 9 | 441 | — | **3.2 s** | 16.1× |
| 7 × 10 × 10 | 710 | — | **34.8 s** | 36.2× |

All results agree with the `scipy`/HiGHS oracle; the 135-test suite passes throughout.

**The operating point is 6 agents / 8 tasks / 9 configs**, where one re-solve costs
**3.2 s** on the self-contained engine — so revalidating a 12-entry cache is ~38 s of
genuinely expensive optimization, against 4.89 ms in the original study.

## 3. Comparators

The earlier study scores CRR against one opponent that re-solves entries it knows are
untouched. The comparator set here, cheapest opponent first:

| strategy | what it does |
|---|---|
| `no_reval` | Keep the stale cache. Not a competitor — it quantifies the safety cost of doing nothing. |
| **`naive_recheck`** | **The primary comparator.** No certificates, no reverse index, no zonotopes: recompute the objective, check the cached choice is still best and still feasible, re-solve only if not. This is CRR's Stage 2 with the vocabulary removed. **CRR's entire claim is that its machinery buys something over this.** |
| `footprint_resolve` | Re-solve only genuinely-touched entries. The competent-engineer baseline. |
| `full_resolve` | Re-solve everything. The paper's baseline, kept for comparability. Also the ground truth. |
| `crr` | The four-stage pipeline. |

### 3.1 Cost accounting

Every strategy reports **energy-model evaluations**, solver calls, re-solves and
wall-clock. Counting only "expensive solver calls" is exactly what allows a Stage-2
check that rebuilds the whole objective to be billed as free. Here, work is work.

## 4. Statistical design (pre-registered)

- **Unit of analysis is the (seed, refinement) cache**, not the entry. Entries within a
  cache share a scenario and are strongly dependent; pooling entry-level rows would
  inflate the effective sample size. Each cache contributes one number per strategy,
  and the bootstrap **resamples seeds**, not entries.
- **One primary endpoint**: median paired log-ratio of **energy-model evaluations**,
  `crr` vs `naive_recheck`, on **Track B**. Everything else is labelled exploratory.
- **Practical-significance margin: ≥ 1.2×.** A detectable 3% win is not a reason to
  carry certificates, a reverse index and zonotopes.
- **Severity is reported as a curve**, never marginalized: every metric is monotone in
  severity, so a single number is a statement about the chosen severity distribution.
- **Validity gates run before any headline.** A run is *void*, not slow, if the cache is
  argmin-homogeneous, if any ground-truth solve hit the node limit (optimality
  unproven), if any strategy falsely certified, or if Track B regressed to a scan.
- **Falsification rule, stated in advance:** if CRR's loss rate against `naive_recheck`
  is 0% at every severity, the severity range is too narrow and the study is invalid.

## 6. Threats to validity

- **The ODD is simulated, not observed.** The AR(1) generator has not been fitted to
  meteorological data and no goodness-of-fit test was performed. Ingesting real METAR /
  ASOS observations is the obvious strengthening and is out of scope here.
- **The energy constants are published, not calibrated.** M0/M1 constants come from the
  existing model and Zeng et al.; roughly a dozen constants are uncalibrated against
  measurement. The per-dimension ablation in §2.2 is the affordable substitute for a
  sensitivity analysis.
- **M1's energy-optimal cruise pins to the top of the speed box in strong headwind.**
  This is physically correct (higher ground speed cuts exposure time), but it means the
  speed dimension is not interior across the whole envelope. The fraction of cached
  entries whose optimum sits on a box rail is reported as a diagnostic.
- **`perturbation_bound` returns `None` for Types I and III.** No sound a-priori bound
  on `|E1_k − E0_k|` for the rotary-wing fidelity step was derived without evaluating
  the model — i.e. without doing the very work the certificate exists to avoid. CRR
  therefore falls through to recomputing, which is why its energy-evaluation count
  matches `naive_recheck`. **Whether a sound, cheap certificate exists for
  assignment-coupled objectives is the open question this evaluation surfaces**, and it
  is the most consequential thing here for the paper.
- **Single-shot refinement.** CRR's amortization argument — many refinements over a
  cache's lifetime — is not tested. That is where its strongest case likely lives, and
  it is future work rather than a claim made here.
- **Fleet size.** The self-contained engine reaches ~7 agents; HiGHS is ~12–36× faster
  and needed beyond that. Results are reported at 6 × 8 × 9.
