\section{Evaluation}
\label{sec:evaluation}

We evaluate CRR against three research questions:
\textbf{RQ1 (Efficiency)}---how effectively does CRR reduce invocations of the
expensive optimizer, relative to full cache revalidation, when the model is
refined? \textbf{RQ2 (Correctness)}---does CRR preserve exact optimality under the
evolved model, and what does the prior no-revalidation approach cost?
\textbf{RQ3 (Sensitivity \& scalability)}---how do the savings vary with
refinement type, footprint size $|\Delta|$, and problem scale?

\subsection{Setup}
We implement CRR on the fleet task-assignment MILP of Section~\ref{sec:motivating}:
$M$ drones cover $J$ tasks under a chosen configuration $k$, minimizing total
fleet energy subject to a per-agent battery-reserve budget
$\mathit{usable}=(1-\rho)\,C/s$. Energy is agent-independent, so the objective is
configuration-determined ($v^\ast=\min_k\sum_j E[k,j]$) while the budget couples
the configuration choice to a bin-packing assignment: tightening it can render the
cheapest configuration infeasible and force a costlier one.

\paragraph{A self-contained optimization engine.} So that CRR's Stage-3 warm
dual-simplex is real rather than emulated, the entire pipeline runs on a
from-scratch solver: a bounded-variable primal/dual revised simplex and an
LP-relaxation branch-and-bound with warm-started dual-simplex children, both
validated against \texttt{scipy.optimize.linprog}/\texttt{milp} over $120$
randomized oracle instances. \emph{No external MILP solver is used.} Stage~3
warm-starts the dual simplex from each entry's stored basis; Stage~4 is the
branch-and-bound.

A cache is populated under $\mathcal{M}_0$ with one proven-optimal entry per
operating regime (ODD), modelled as a wind factor scaling energy from \emph{calm}
to \emph{strong}; each entry stores its certificate, dependency set, validity
ranges, and the bin-packing LP basis used to warm-start repairs, and is inserted
into the reverse index. We then apply the three refinements---Type~II (tightened
battery reserve), Type~III (tighter objective linearization), Type~I (a new
humidity factor)---and revalidate, comparing against \emph{full revalidation}
(re-solve every entry; the naive $N$-solve upper bound and optimality ground
truth) and \emph{no revalidation} (the stale $\mathcal{M}_0$ cache). All runs are
deterministic.

\subsection{RQ1: Efficiency}
On a cache of $N{=}16$ entries, a representative refinement of each type is
revalidated with \emph{zero} MILP re-solves (Table~\ref{tab:crr-efficiency}), a
$100\%$ reduction versus full revalidation, at exact optimality: entries are
discharged through the cheap stages (for Type~II, $1$ certifies and $15$ are
warm-repaired; Types~III and~I split across S2/S3 with no re-solve). As severity
grows the population escalates through the cost-ordered stages
(Fig.~\ref{fig:crr-stages}): for Type~II the split moves
$S2/S3/S4 = 10/6/0 \to 1/15/0 \to 0/10/6 \to 0/4/12$, and the re-solve reduction
degrades gracefully from $100\%$ to $25\%$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:crr-reduction})---CRR
invokes the optimizer only for entries whose integer optimum genuinely changed,
and never does worse than full revalidation. Type~III holds at $100\%$ across the
range: a tighter linearization preserves the configuration ranking, so every
optimum is corrected by a certificate or a warm re-assignment.

The Stage-3 repairs realize the paper's cut-warmed repair: the stored basis
warm-starts a dual-simplex re-optimization of the bin-packing LP after the battery
RHS tightens. Across the Type-II sweep the warm re-solves take $132$ pivots in
total versus $2\,971$ cold---a $22\times$ reduction (Fig.~\ref{fig:crr-pivots};
$22$ vs.\ $762$ at $\rho{=}0.54$). The warm basis is already dual-feasible, so its
pivot count barely grows with problem size while the cold count climbs, widening
the advantage with scale.

\subsection{RQ2: Correctness}
Across all $15$ refinement points CRR is \emph{sound}: the optimality gap against
the full-revalidation ground truth is $0.0$ (the engine is exact arithmetic),
confirming Theorem~\ref{thm:soundness} at $\tau_{\mathrm{req}}{=}0$. The prior
no-revalidation approach is unsafe under model evolution: the stale cache leaves
up to $100\%$ of entries \emph{infeasible} under the tightened Type-II constraint
(up to $56\%$ under Type~III)---cached plans that now violate the safety reserve.
The dominant cost of staleness is constraint violation, precisely the
safety-relevant error CRR eliminates without a full re-solve.

\subsection{RQ3: Sensitivity and scalability}
A regime-scoped Type-II refinement touching $k$ of $16$ regimes is served by the
reverse index: entries outside the footprint are reused at Stage~1 without
examination, so the number examined tracks $|\Delta|$
(Fig.~\ref{fig:crr-footprint}), while the number of expensive re-solves saturates
at $3$ even when $|\Delta|$ touches all $16$ entries. Sweeping fleet size
$M\in\{3,5,8\}$ and cache size $N\in\{8,16,24\}$, CRR sustains a $100\%$ re-solve
reduction, and the warm-start advantage grows with scale: the Stage-3 cold LP
pivot count rises with the fleet ($\approx 593\to 854\to 1234$ for $M=3,5,8$)
while the warm dual-simplex stays flat at $\approx 20$ pivots.

\subsection{Threats to validity}
The pipeline runs entirely on the self-contained engine (no external solver);
every interaction is counted and typed (none / arithmetic S2 / warm-LP S3 /
branch-and-bound S4). Stage~3 is a genuine warm dual-simplex re-optimization; when
a cheap rounding of its LP solution cannot close the repair, the engine confirms
the optimum exactly. The engine is correctness-focused rather than
speed-optimized, so on these small instances wall-clock is comparable to full
revalidation; we therefore report the algorithmic metrics (solver-call and pivot
reduction), which are properties of the mechanism and would translate to
wall-clock on a production solver. The study is a controlled instantiation of the
running example: absolute magnitudes depend on instance size, but the stage
distribution, the soundness result, and the footprint/scale trends are properties
of CRR.
